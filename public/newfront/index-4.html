Astigmatism

Astigmatism is a common and generally treatable imperfection in the curvature of the eye that causes blurred distance and near vision.

Astigmatism is a common eye condition that causes blurred or distorted vision. It occurs when the cornea (the clear front surface of the eye) or the lens inside the eye has an irregular shape, causing light to be refracted in different directions.

People with astigmatism may experience symptoms such as blurry or distorted vision, difficulty seeing at night, eyestrain, headaches, and squinting. These symptoms can be mild or severe and may affect one or both eyes..

Treatment for astigmatism typically involves prescription eyeglasses or contact lenses that correct the irregular shape of the cornea or lens. In some cases, surgery may be an option to reshape the cornea and improve vision.

Symptoms Of Astigmatism

  • Blurry or distorted vision, especially when looking at objects up close or far away

  • Eye strain or discomfort, especially after reading or using a computer for an extended period of time

  • Headaches, especially around the forehead or temples

  • Difficulty seeing at night or in low light conditions

  • Sensitivity to glare or bright lights

  • Eye fatigue or tiredness.
